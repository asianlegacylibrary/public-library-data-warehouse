TIBETAN CHRONOLOGICAL TABLES
                                  Adopted From
     TIBETAN CHRONOLOGICAL TABLES OF JAM DBYANGS BZAD PA & SUM PA MKHAN PO    
      By Alaka Chattopadhyaya, Central Institute of Higher Tibetan Studies
      Sarnath, Varanasi, India, P.xi-xiii.
                                      NOTE:
       Originally, if the Tibetan BON scriptures are to be believed, accor-
    ding to Namkai Norbu (DRUNG, DRU AND BON, P.147 ff.), the "5 element-
    12 Animal" cycle or `Cycle of Mewa' was taught by official BON's founder
    Shenrab Miwoche, and he even "went to China and taught the Chinese...
    the method of calculating the `Black and White Mewa' according to the
    Chinese astrological system... The best known is the combination of the
    years in a 180 year cycle subdivided in to three 60 year cycles... Thus,
    combining the white `Mewa' number one with the first year of the cycle,
    that is, the Wood-Mouse-Male year, counting forward or backward, only
    after three 60 year cycles, that is, after 180 years, does there again 
    recur the white `Mewa' number one combine with the Wood-Mouse-Male year."
    Therefore, Namkhai Norbu holds this system was a astrological teaching
    which spread from Shang Shung to the Central Asia in general, and to the
    Central Tibetan Kingdom in particular.
       However, according to S.C. Das (AN INTRODUCTION TO THE GRAMMAR OF
    TIBETAN LANGUAGE, P.xi), "These Tibetan names correspond to the Chinese
    names of the twelve animals and are said to have been first introduced
    in Tibet by Queen Wenchang, daughter of Emporor T'ai Tsung, who was
    married to King MI'I LHA, surnamed SRONG BTZAN GAM PO, in about 635 (or
    641) A.D." According to (A.H. Francke, ANTIQUITIES OF INDIAN TIBET, VOL.
    II: P.84) the Mongolian Religious History, the Bodhim”r states (pp.841)
    during the reign of SRONG BTZAN GAM PO, "the calendar (was) introduced
    from China." This early usage is indirectly confirmed by the ROYAL ANNALS 
    OF TIBET starting to use the twelve `Animal' system in 650/51 A.D.,
    "the year of the Dog," and H.E. Richardson (ANCIENT HISTORICAL EDICTS AT
    LHASA, P.47) says, "the Tun Huang Annals, which cover the period A.D. 650-
    763, use the regular series of animal names, without differentiation of
    male and female or the addition of the five elements,..."
       Das further says (ibit, P.xi), "About a century later the system of
    astrology then prevalent in China, which contained a cycle of sixty
    years, was introduced in Tibet." This was according to Bunkyo Aoki
    (STUDY ON EARLY TIBETAN CHRONICLES, P.14) during "the reign of KHRI
    LDE GTZUG BRTAN MES AAG TSOMS (705-755 A.D.),... the Chinese Princess,
    Kim Shing Kong jo, arrived in Tibet (707-710 A.D.) according to the
    RDO RINGS inscription."
       This is confirmed by the PADMA BKA' THANG (Toussaint's version trans-
    lated by Kenneth Douglas & Gyendolyn Bays, P. 350) which says, "King Triday
    Tsogtan (BLUE ANNALS, P.xix, KHRI LDE GTZUG BRTAN, r.705-755 A.D.) who
    had adopted the rules of Chinese astrology, protected the Tibetans by
    means of astrology...., in the first half of the tenth month... (P.351)
    in the horse year, the first spring month, the first day of the Tiger 
    month," it seems likely that they accepted their `60 year' cycle too. 
       Namkai Norbu (DRUNG, DRU AND BON, P.150) says, it was also in
    "Od%d%iyƒna, the small kingdom north-west of India (modern Swat in
    Pakistan) to which tradition ascribes the origin of the RDZOGS CHEN
    teaching... In fact, in the principal Tantra of the `Secret Instructions'
    (MAN NGAG) Series of RDZOGS CHEN, `The Sound That Goes Beyond,' (SGAR
    THAL 'GYUR CHEN PO'I RGYUD) we read: `In the relation to the five
    elements and the twelve animals... are unfolded the astrological
    calculations of the elements.'"
       King KHRI LDE SRONG BTZAN RAL PA CAN, on his stone pillar commemorat-
    ing his peace treaty with China outside the LHA SA JO KHANG, uses the
    system three times: to signify its signing in China (`Female-Iron-Ox'/
    821 A.D.), its signing in Lhasa (`Male-Water-Tiger'/ 822 A.D.), and the
    erection of its treaty on a stone pillar on the border between China and
    Tibet (`Female-Water-Hare'/ 823 A.D.). This proves it was in use by then.
       S.C. Das (AN INTRODUCTION TO THE GRAMMAR OF TIBETAN LANGUAGE, P.xi) 
    says "At a later period... with the introduction of the Kƒlacakra Tantras,
    the Indian Cycle of sixty years called the `virhaspati chakra' was brought 
    to Tibet from ShrŒ dhƒnya kat%aka (DPAL LDAN 'BRAS SPUNGS=Sanchi Topes?),
    situated on the River Kr%is%n%ƒ near modern Amraoti, in Southern India."
       Furthermore, S.C. Das (ibit, P.xvii) says "With the year called
    Prabhava in which Shƒkya is believed to have been born, the date of intro-
    duction of the Tibetan RAB BYUNG cycle begins. Tibetan historians affirm
    that Kƒlachakra Tantras and the `vrihaspati' cycle were brought from
    Kƒshmir by an Indian Budhist named Tsi lu pan%d%ita (TZI LU PAn dI TA)."
       Bunkyo Aoki says (STUDY ON EARLY TIBETAN CHRONICLES, P.13), "We learn
    by comparing the `vrihaspati chakra' to the Chinese sexagenary cycles
    that they both rotate with the cycle of 60 years, and that the `vrihaspati
    chakra has a distinct name for each year of the series, while the Chinese
    sexagenary cycle has the combination of 12 animals and 5 elements for
    making up the cycle of 60 years, and also that the `vrihaspati chakra' 
    commences with the `Fire-Female-Hare' year (the 4th in the series) of
    the Chinese 60-year cycle, which begins (3 years earlier) with the year
    of `Tree-Male-Mouse' (1024 A.D.)."
       Bunkyo Aoki says (STUDY ON EARLY TIBETAN CHRONICLES, P.21), "that
    `Prabhava' or RAB BYUNG corresponds to the year of `Fire-Female-Hare'
    with which the RAB BYUNG cycle commences, and that the `Fire-Female-Hare'
    year falls definitly upon the year 1027 A.D."
       Therefore, given that this `cycle' could have started earlier than
    in 1027, I have additionally applied in reverse the 60 year cycle to the
    10 previous cycles,
